Professional Summary

I am a passionate robotics engineer and graduate research assistant at Johns Hopkins University, specializing in medical robotics, autonomous systems, and computer vision. With a strong foundation in mechanical engineering and advanced studies in robotics, I focus on developing innovative solutions for complex robotic applications including surgical robotics, autonomous navigation, and precision positioning systems.

I'm passionate about pushing the boundaries of what's possible in robotics through innovative design and cutting-edge algorithms.

Optimal Motion Planning for UR5-Based Steering Wheel Installation

Developed a custom C++ PRM motion planner for a UR5, constructing a collision-free roadmap in 6D C-space with radian-distance edge connections, used FLANN KD-tree nearest neighbors and Dijkstra for efficient path search. Integrated with MoveIt2 for real-time collision checking, achieving 30s planning time in cluttered automotive workcells for installing steering wheels on the vehicle.

Manipulation of UR5 Robotic Arm

Programmed a UR5 to execute a "place-and-draw" skill, teaching start/end waypoints and drew two 5 cm parallel lines with 10 cm spacing. Designed the pipeline to be rotation-invariant with respect to the initial point orientation. Implemented and compared three MATLAB controllers—analytical IK (DH + Paden–Kahan with optimal selection among 8 solutions), Resolved-Rate, and Jacobian Transpose.

UR5 MATLAB Inverse Kinematics Manipulation
